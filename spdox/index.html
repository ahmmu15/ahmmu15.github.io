Behind the scenes

Most of my opinions, were based on the common patterns that spread all over the internet. Following a well-known pattern considers best practice, since it saves you time and efforts when it comes to user educating.

Most users will be already familiar with the purpose and the actions behind each components, however, sometimes, that might not be the case, and that’s when (testing) comes in action.

It might also be a great idea to come up with a new pattern, everyone wants to be unique and be the pioneer in the field, and that’s amazing, in case you have the required time and efforts to do so.

For this task, I sticked with what already there, and didn’t try to reinvent the wheel, thus, I expect you guys, are familiar as well with what I came up with, for this reason, I will be concise with my explanation.

Navigation bar

As you can notice, nothing new here, almost the same, with some repolishing. Icons replaced with thin line icons, somehow these icons can give a modern look to the interface.

Not just that, but the language has added there! Why? Because it’s a common pattern, and most of users will expect to find it there! I was surprised to find it in the footer! When I first visited your website, I thought you don't support other languages!

Carousel

They say that this is the place that you can use to capture user’s attention! And that's obviously true, since it’s the first thing that will appear on the screen.

Capturing user attention is not that easy, specially when things get repetitive! I mean, come on! Let’s be honest! Carousels and sliders are everywhere, and the way they behave can be expected! Slide, slide aaand then slide, right?

Repetitive things can be ignored unconsciously by our brain! Just remember how our eyes ignore the nose, like it’s not there anymore!

Fortunately, our eyes are very good at spotting any movement out there, this ability is an essential to our survival, and we, humans, keep it in our kit.

That’s why I came up with the subtle animation of the t-shirt, because animation is the only thing available for us on the web, to create some kind of movement.

Too much animation can be overwhelming though, that’s why I tried my best to keep it subtle, all over the web page.

Best selling

This section looks like still, but it can move (aka. slide), disabling the autoplay to create a difference between different parts of the website. But still, there’s a chance to make it move, by pressing the navigation arrows on its sides.

Hovering over one card, brings a new component, where user can like, add to cart, and share. Same component is there by default on mobile, since there’s no hover state in touch devices.

Stay updated

This one moved out of the footer as well, because this part deserves better attention, not just because it can boost up company email marketing, but also telling users that the company is good enough to keep its users updated with latest.

This can give a feeling of care, and whenever the user feels that you care about him/ her, you gonna guarantee verbal marketing!
